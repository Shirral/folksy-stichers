Running Stitch

The running stitch is a simple and versatile embroidery stitch that holds a special place in the world of needlework. You can use it to decorate the edges and seams of your clothing and accessories, make delicate outlines in your designs, or to create Japanese sashiko. Whether used as a foundation for more complex stitches or as a standalone design element, the running stitch is a fundamental technique that every embroidery enthusiast should master.

The running stitch is formed by passing the needle in and out of the fabric in a continuous motion, creating a dashed line effect. Watch the video to see exactly how to do it!

Back Stitch

Back stitch is a fundamental embroidery stitch that offers both simplicity and versatility. It is used to create precise outlines, intricate details, and sturdy seams. Unlike most stitches, backstitch creates a solid line that is ideal for defining shapes and adding textural elements.

Its simplicity lies in its straightforward technique: bring the needle up from the fabric, take a small backward stitch, and then bring the needle forward again, repeating the process to form a continuous line. Watch the video to see exactly how to do it!

Satin Stitch

Satin stitch is a lustrous and elegant embroidery technique that adds a smooth, satin-like finish to any design. This stitch is cherished for its ability to create solid, filled areas with a polished appearance, and can be used to embellish any shape you want - flowers, letters, or any element that requires a vibrant and smooth texture.

By closely placing parallel stitches side by side, the fabric beneath is entirely covered, resulting in a seamless and dense surface. Watch the video to see exactly how to do it!

Cross-stitch

Cross-stitching is a beloved embroidery technique that has captivated stitchers for centuries with its charm and versatility. It is characterized by the formation of X-shaped stitches that create intricate patterns and detailed designs. Crafty fans of retro games treasure it as it's easy to bring their favourite pixel characters to life with them. Pokemon-themed cushion cover? Why not!

Each stitch is formed by crossing the needle over the intersection of the fabric's weave, resulting in a distinctive textured effect. Watch the video to see exactly how to do it!

Lazy Daisy Stitch

The lazy daisy stitch, also known as the detached chain stitch, is a delightful embroidery technique that brings whimsy and charm to any design. It's named for its petal-like appearance, resembling a daisy flower or a leaf - and so, embroidering these are its main uses. However, its lovely, dimensional loop will add a playful and organic touch to any embroidery!

To create the stitch, bring the needle up through the fabric, form a loose loop, and anchor it back down with a small stitch at the end. Watch the video to see exactly how to do it!

Woven Wheel Stitch

The woven wheel stitch, also known as the woven rose or woven spider wheel, is a captivating embroidery technique that brings intricate beauty to your designs. This stitch is characterized by a circular arrangement of spokes that are woven together to create a dimensional, textured wheel-like pattern. It is often used to create stunning floral motifs, such as roses, or decorative wheels.

To create the stitch, start with a central spoke and then weave thread over and under the spokes in a circular fashion, gradually filling the area. Watch the video to see exactly how to do it!

Chain Stitch

The chain stitch is a classic and versatile embroidery stitch that adds a delicate and graceful touch to any design. It is characterized by a series of interlocking loops that form a chain-like pattern. This stitch is widely used for outlining, creating borders, and adding decorative elements. With its smooth and flowing appearance, the chain stitch can also be used to fill shapes or create textured surfaces.

To create the stitch, bring the needle up through the fabric and then insert it back into the same hole, forming a loop. Repeat the process, catching the thread within each loop, to continue the chain. Watch the video to see exactly how to do it!

French Knot

The French knot is a delightful embroidery technique that adds texture, dimension, and a touch of elegance to your designs. This stitch is characterized by small, raised knots that resemble tiny beads or pearls. It is often used to create decorative accents, flower centers, or intricate details.

To create a French knot, bring the needle up through the fabric and then wrap the thread around the needle a few times, holding the loops taut. Insert the needle back into the fabric near the starting point, keeping the loops intact, and gently pull the thread through to form a snug knot. Watch the video to see exactly how to do it!
